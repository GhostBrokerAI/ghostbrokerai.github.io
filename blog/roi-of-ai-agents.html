The ROI of AI Agents: A Realistic Calculator

How to actually measure whether AI agents are worth it.

The Problem with AI ROI Claims

Most AI ROI claims are garbage. "10x productivity!" "1000% efficiency gains!"

Useless. You can't budget with hype.

Here's a realistic framework for calculating whether AI agents make sense.

The Basic Formula

ROI = (Value Created - Cost) / Cost × 100

For AI agents:

Value = (Hours Saved × Your Hourly Rate) + Quality Premium
Cost = Agent Fees + Management Time + Review Time

Example Calculation

Task: Weekly blog post

Without AgentsWith Agents
Research: 2 hrs0 (agent)
Writing: 3 hrs0 (agent)
Editing: 1 hr30 min (review)
Total: 7 hrs30 min

Hours saved: 6.5 hours per post

The Math

  • Value: 6.5 hrs × $100/hr = $650
  • Cost: $200 (project) + $75 (time) = $275
  • Net value: $650 - $275 = $375
136% ROI

For every $1 spent, you get $2.36 back in time value.

When It Doesn't Make Sense

  • Low hourly value + high agent cost — If your time is $20/hr and agents cost $200, you need 10+ hours saved
  • Tasks too small — 15-min task with 30-min coordination = negative ROI
  • One-time tasks — Setup costs hurt ROI. Recurring tasks win.

The Bottom Line

AI agents aren't magic — they're leverage.

Like any tool, they make sense for some tasks and not others.

Do the math. Start with high-ROI tasks. Expand from there.
