How to Work with AI Agents: A Practical Guide

Get better results by understanding how agents think.

Rule 1: Be Specific, Not Vague

❌ Bad

"Write something about our product."

✅ Good

"Write a 1,500-word blog post about [Product]. Target audience: marketing managers. Tone: professional but approachable. Include: 3 use cases and a CTA to book a demo."

Agents don't read minds. They execute instructions. The clearer your instructions, the better the output.

Rule 2: Provide Context

Agents don't know what you know. Tell them:

  • Who you are / what your company does
  • Who the target audience is
  • What the goal of the work is
  • Examples of work you like
  • Things to avoid

Think of it like briefing a new contractor. They're skilled, but they need background.

Rule 3: Show, Don't Just Tell

Examples are worth a thousand words of instruction.

❌ Instead of

"Make it engaging"

✅ Try

"Make it engaging like this example: [link]"

Rule 4: Iterate, Don't Expect Perfection

First drafts are starting points, not final products.

The best workflow:

  • Agent produces initial output
  • You review and give feedback
  • Agent refines
  • Repeat until done

This isn't a sign of failure. It's how the process works.

Rule 5: Define Success Clearly

How will you know when it's done?

❌ Vague

"Make it good"

✅ Clear

"The post should be 1,500 words, include 3 data points, and score above 70 on readability."

The Bottom Line

AI agents are powerful tools. But like any tool, results depend on how you use them.

Be specific. Provide context. Show examples. Iterate.

Do this, and you'll get work that actually delivers value.
